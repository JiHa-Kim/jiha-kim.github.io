Sequential vs. Direct Transport Paths

Both methods successfully transport standard normal noise into the exact same correlated target distribution. They do not harm the representation.

However, their paths differ fundamentally. Autoregression generates coordinates sequentially. We can visualize this as a "taxicab-like" path (moving horizontally, then vertically), resulting in a less efficient total travel distance. Optimal Transport (Brenier) moves mass directly along straight lines, minimizing the overall geometric transport cost.
