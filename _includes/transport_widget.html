Whole-Bag Transport Without Splitting

Source $A$ and source $B$ each carry one sealed $1\text{ kg}$ bag, while destinations $C$ and $D$ each need $1\text{ kg}$. Because the bags cannot be torn open, the only feasible plans are the two possible assignments of whole bags to destinations.

Indivisibility collapses the problem to a tiny discrete search: compare the uncrossed assignment with the crossed assignment and pick the cheaper permutation.
There are only two feasible whole-bag assignments, and the uncrossed one is strictly cheaper. This is the smallest discrete picture of why uncrossed structure keeps appearing in optimal transport.
